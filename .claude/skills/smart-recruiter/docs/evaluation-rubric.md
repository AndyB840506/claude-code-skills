# Evaluation Rubric

Use this when scoring candidates. Internal only — never share with candidate.

## Scoring Dimensions

### 1. Must-Have Requirements Met (40% weight)

**10** — Covers all must-haves with clear evidence and experience  
**7–9** — Covers most must-haves; minor gaps easily addressed  
**5–6** — Covers some must-haves; notable gaps that could be trained  
**2–4** — Covers few must-haves; significant gaps  
**0–1** — Missing critical must-haves

### 2. Cultural Fit (20% weight)

**10** — Strong alignment with team values, work style, and company culture  
**7–9** — Good alignment; minor preference differences  
**5–6** — Neutral; can adapt with guidance  
**2–4** — Some misalignment; may struggle with culture  
**0–1** — Poor cultural fit

### 3. Motivation & Expectations Alignment (20% weight)

**10** — Motivated, expectations align perfectly with offer  
**7–9** — Motivated, expectations align well  
**5–6** — Moderately motivated, expectations align on major points  
**2–4** — Motivation unclear or expectations diverge on key points  
**0–1** — Unmotivated or expectations misaligned

### 4. Communication & Soft Skills (20% weight)

**10** — Excellent communication, strong interpersonal skills  
**7–9** — Good communication, solid soft skills  
**5–6** — Adequate communication, acceptable soft skills  
**2–4** — Weak communication or limited soft skills  
**0–1** — Poor communication; significant soft skill gaps

## Verdict Thresholds

- **Strong Fit** (8.0–10): Recommend to advance to next round
- **Possible Fit** (5.0–7.9): Advance with reservations; may need additional assessment
- **Not a Fit** (0–4.9): Do not recommend advancement

## Notes for Scoring

- Score based on EVIDENCE from the interview, not assumptions
- If dimension wasn't fully explored, note it in the report
- Cultural fit is subjective but should be based on specific statements
- Communication can be assessed throughout the interview (clarity, listening, engagement)
