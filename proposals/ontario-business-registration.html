1. What You Need to Register

Before submitting the Ontario Chamber of Commerce grant application, Kuma Group must have an active Ontario business registration. There are two practical options at this stage:

Option What it is Cost Time Best for
Business Name Registration ★ Recommended first step Registers "Kuma Group" as a business name. You operate as yourselves, doing business as Kuma Group. Filed online through the Ontario Business Registry. ~$60 CAD (online at ontario.ca/businessregistry) Same day (online processing) Fast start; acceptable for many grants. Does not create a separate legal entity.
Ontario Business Corporation Incorporates Kuma Group as a legal entity under the Ontario Business Corporations Act (OBCA). Provides limited liability and looks more formal to grant reviewers. ~$300 CAD filing fee + optional legal fees ($500–$1,500) 1–3 business days (online) Stronger for grants and investors. Recommended if you plan to scale or take on outside investment.
Recommendation
Register the business name first (~$60 online through the Ontario Business Registry at ontario.ca/businessregistry) to have a valid Business Identification Number for the grant application. Hugo Lancheros, based in Ontario, handles the registration as the primary local contact. In parallel, consult an Ontario lawyer or accountant about incorporation — particularly if this grant is approved, as incorporated entities are treated more favourably in future funding rounds.

4. Pre-Submission Checklist

Complete these steps before submitting the grant application:

  • Decide on registration type
    Ontario Business Name Registration (~$60 online, same day at ontario.ca/businessregistry) for speed — or Ontario corporation under the OBCA (~$300 online) for stronger credibility. Business name registration is acceptable for most grants; incorporation is better long-term.
  • Hugo registers through the Ontario Business Registry
    Go to ontario.ca/businessregistry. Hugo completes the registration as the Ontario-based registrant. Have government-issued ID and Hugo's Ontario mailing address ready. Fee ~$60 payable online by credit card.
  • Receive Ontario Business Identification Number
    Issued immediately upon successful online registration. Add it to the grant proposal header where marked ⚠.
  • Hugo fills in his bio section in the grant proposal
    2–3 sentences about background, sales/marketing experience, and relevance to Ontario SMEs.
  • Hugo confirms his Ontario mailing address for the proposal
    Update the grant proposal header and this document with Hugo's confirmed Ontario address.
  • Prepare simple financial statement
    One-page summary: current assets, any Hiresignal revenue or self-funding amount, projected costs. May be required by the Chamber.
  • Confirm grant program requirements with the Ontario Chamber
    Verify whether the specific grant program accepts pending registrations or requires an established Business Identification Number at submission time.
  • Remove DRAFT banners from grant proposal before final submission
    Both the yellow draft banner and all ⚠ internal notes must be removed or resolved before the proposal is submitted.
